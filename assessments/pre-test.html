How to use this page

This is a diagnostic pre-test, not an exam. There is no penalty for a low score.

🎯
Why we do it

We capture a baseline today (Session 1). At the very end of the course (Session 12) you take the post-test — the same kind of questions — and the difference is the growth you can point to.

✅
How to answer

Answer from what you actually know. If you have never seen Kotlin or Compose, that is completely expected — "I don't know" is useful data. Make your best guess rather than leaving it blank, but don't look anything up while taking it.

📚
It becomes a study guide

After you press Submit, every question reveals a full explanation. Come back here at the end of each week — the explanations are a fast self-check, and you will be surprised how much obvious it all looks by Week 4.

Scoring

The passing threshold is set intentionally low (40%) because this is a diagnostic — a "pass" here just means you walked in with some prior exposure. Your real target is to beat this score on the post-test. The short answer is matched case-insensitively against a small set of accepted words, so write the single key term, not a sentence.

Answers stay hidden until you submit

Choose an answer for each of the 19 questions, then press Submit answers at the bottom. You will get a per-question verdict, an explanation, your total score, and a Retry button.
