How to become the strongest engineer in your cohort

Four habits, backed by how learning actually works. Run them every session.

⚡
The whole secret in one line

Talent is just compounded reps. The strongest engineer in any room isn't the one with the best memory — it's the one who practiced deliberately, recalled from memory, spaced it over days, and shipped real things. Do these four on this map and you will pull ahead.

🎯

Habit 1Deliberate practice

Don't re-read what's easy — attack the edge of what you can't quite do yet. Pick the next locked-feeling node, do its challenges until ALL TESTS PASSED, then make it harder: change a requirement, break it on purpose, refactor it cleaner. Comfort is a signal to level up.

🧠

Habit 2Retrieve, don't review

Close the notes and pull the answer from memory — that struggle is what builds the wiring. Predict the lifecycle callbacks before you run them; write the remember line from scratch before you peek. Each quiz and flashcard here is a retrieval rep, and your accuracy quietly feeds your mastery.

📆

Habit 3Space it out

Cramming feels productive and forgets fast. Touch each topic on several different days — even 15 minutes. The spaced-repetition decks resurface a card right as you're about to forget it, which is the single most efficient way to make knowledge permanent. Watch your due reviews below; clearing them is the win.

🛠️

Habit 4Build real things

Tutorials teach recognition; projects teach engineering. After every node, wire what you learned into a screen of your own app. The whole map points at one outcome — a real Android app that stores data locally and talks to a live API. Ship small, ship often, and let the final project be the thing you actually defend.
